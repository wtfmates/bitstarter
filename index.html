CityWatch is a website and mobile app that watches the economy on a smaller scale. Scraping data from various real estate and rental websites, Craigslist advertisements for job openings and housing, local classified ads, and even Twitter mentions, it will give you a smaller scale and more personalized view of the economy. All of this information will come together in simple, easy to read statists and graphs, that will come together to show you which cities are on the rise or on the fall, and how quickly that rise or fall is happening. This will enable both persons and business to make more informed real estate investments and relocation choices.

The data presented to you is completely personalized and customizable. You see what is important to you. If you are a college graduate, looking to move to an area that has a high demand for workers in your chosen profession, you can filter cities by the growth rate of demand for workers in your field, or just the amount of job postings, giving you an insightful look into the parts of the country where you will be most likely to be successful. If you are the manager or CEO of a company, looking to open a branch or store in a new location, you will be able to filter cities by employment rate, economic growth, and the amount of new businesses opening, allowing you to filter through and avoid cities that may be experiencing an economic stagnation that could cause your new branch to fail.

All of this will be shown via simple and easy to interpret charts and statistics, either online or through our mobile app. Access to the website and some basic information will be free, but for more indepth looks at cities or for customized searches, you will have to pay a monthly subscription of 14.99. Lifetime and yearly subscription options will be available as well, but pricing is still being decided upon. The mobile app will be available for iOS and Android for a single purchase of 9.99, with no subscription needed, but it won't provide you with as much depth and customization as the more full featured web-based app does.
